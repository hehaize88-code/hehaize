How to review this category

Use garment measurements instead of a familiar size label.

01Shoulder, chest, sleeve and length02Collar, button and cuff QC03Protect structured finishes

Long sleeves, polos, shirts and cardigans need a broader comparison than a generic tops label. Each of the eight research pages records one catalog destination, one source ID and one price observation. On the live page, confirm the precise garment type, collar or closure, color and option because grouped listings can combine products with different construction and sizing.

Compare shoulder, chest, sleeve and body length in centimetres with a garment that already fits. For buttoned or collared items, warehouse images should show placket alignment, collar shape, cuffs and straight seams. Knit pieces also benefit from a clear view of surface pulls or uneven edges. Most tops consolidate well with other soft goods, but rigid buttons, collars and textured finishes should not be crushed simply to reduce a small amount of parcel volume.

Warehouse QC focus

A flat, straight garment view makes sleeve and body proportions easier to assess. Request one measurement at a time when the existing image cannot answer the fit question.
